Costa Rica Itinerary (9 Days)

Pura Vida — Rainforests, Volcanoes & Pacific Coast

San José → Manuel Antonio → Monteverde → La Fortuna / Arenal → Tamarindo
← Back to Itineraries
Day 1 Arrival San José → Drive to Manuel Antonio San José → Manuel Antonio
Arrival
  • Land at Juan Santamaría International Airport (SJO) (allow 1–2 hr for immigration)
  • Pick up rental car at airport — 4WD recommended (~45 min)
  • Drive San José → Manuel Antonio via Route 27 & 34 (~3 hr, 180 km)
  • Optional stop in Jacó for lunch (~45 min)

⚠ Rental Car Tip: Book in advance and choose a reputable company (Adobe, Alamo, Budget). Full insurance coverage strongly recommended. A 4WD/AWD vehicle is essential for dirt roads in Monteverde and Arenal.

Afternoon / Evening
  • Check into hotel in Manuel Antonio (~20 min)
  • Walk to Playa Espadilla or Biesanz for sunset (~1 hr)
  • Dinner at El Avión or La Luna (~1.5 hr)

Day 2 Manuel Antonio National Park + Beach Manuel Antonio
Morning
  • Enter Manuel Antonio National Park early (opens 7 AM) (~3–4 hr)
  • Hike trail to Playa Manuel Antonio — best beach in the park
  • Spot wildlife: sloths, capuchin monkeys, iguanas, coatis
  • Guided nature tour recommended for wildlife spotting (~2 hr)

Park Tip: Tickets must be reserved online in advance at sinac.go.cr. Park entry is limited and sells out — book at least 2–3 days ahead.

Afternoon
  • Beach time at Playa Espadilla (~2 hr)
  • Lunch at Soda Sanchez or El Wagon (~1 hr)
Evening
  • Sunset cocktails at a hillside bar (~1 hr)
  • Dinner at Emilio's Café or Barba Roja (~1.5 hr)
Alternative
  • Ocean kayaking or snorkeling tour (~2–3 hr)
  • White-water rafting on Río Savegre (~half day)

Day 3 Drive to Monteverde — Cloud Forest Arrival Manuel Antonio → Monteverde
Morning
  • Check out of hotel and pack car (~30 min)
  • Drive Manuel Antonio → Monteverde via Route 27 & 1 (~4–5 hr, 250 km)
  • Final stretch on dirt mountain road — 4WD essential (~45 min)

🛣 Road Warning: The last 35 km into Monteverde (via Sardinal) is unpaved and bumpy. Take it slow — road can be rough but is passable in 4WD. Allow extra time.

Afternoon
  • Check in to lodge in Santa Elena (~20 min)
  • Walk around Santa Elena town, grab coffee (~1 hr)
  • Visit Monteverde Cheese Factory (~45 min)
Evening
  • Night walk in the cloud forest — guided tour (~2 hr)
  • Dinner at Morpho's Restaurant or Chimera (~1 hr)

Day 4 Zipline + Cloud Forest Reserve Monteverde
Morning — Zipline
  • Selvatura Park or 100% Aventura zipline tour — some of the longest zip lines in Costa Rica (~3 hr)
  • Superman cable line, Tarzan swing, hanging bridges combo available
  • Book the first slot to beat crowds

Top Zipline Operators: Selvatura Park, 100% Aventura, Extremo Park, and Sky Adventures all operate in the area. Compare included features (bridges, swing, platform count).

Afternoon — Cloud Forest
  • Monteverde Cloud Forest Biological Reserve (~2–3 hr)
  • Hike the Chomogo Trail for quetzal sightings
  • Lunch at a soda in Santa Elena (~1 hr)
Evening
  • Sunset at Mirador San Gerardo viewpoint (~45 min)
  • Dinner in Santa Elena (~1 hr)
Alternative
  • Curi-Cancha Reserve (better for birdwatching) (~2 hr)
  • Hanging bridges at Selvatura (~2 hr)

Day 5 Drive to La Fortuna — Arenal Arrival Monteverde → La Fortuna / Arenal
Morning
  • Check out of hotel and load car (~30 min)
  • Option A: Drive via Tilarán — scenic but longer (~3.5 hr)
  • Option B: Jeep-Boat-Jeep transfer across Lake Arenal (~3 hr, ~$30/person)

Jeep-Boat-Jeep: The classic adventure transfer across Lago Arenal — a shared jeep to the lake, a boat crossing, then another jeep to La Fortuna. Scenic and worth it.

Afternoon
  • Check in to hotel or lodge near Arenal Volcano (~20 min)
  • First views of Arenal Volcano (best on clear afternoons)
  • Walk around La Fortuna town, grab lunch (~1 hr)
  • La Fortuna Waterfall hike — 500+ steps down and back up (~1.5 hr)
Evening
  • Dinner at Don Rufino or Néctar (~1.5 hr)

Day 6 Arenal Volcano Hike + ATV + Hot Springs La Fortuna / Arenal
Morning — Volcano
  • Arenal Volcano National Park — entry and hike (~3–4 hr)
  • Sendero Los Tucanes trail (2.4 km) for best volcano views
  • Sendero Coladas trail passes old 1968 lava fields
  • Arrive early — volcano views often obscured by clouds after noon
Afternoon
  • Lunch near the park (~1 hr)
  • ATV tour through jungle and volcanic terrain (~2–3 hr)
  • OR Canyoning / rappelling waterfalls (~3 hr)
Evening — Hot Springs
  • Hot Springs — soak in volcanic thermal pools at sunset (~2–3 hr)
  • Top options: Baldi Hot Springs (affordable) or Tabacon Grand Spa (upscale, includes dinner)
  • Dinner at the hot springs or in La Fortuna (~1–1.5 hr)

Hot Springs Tip: Tabacon is pricier (~$90+) but stunning — includes dinner. Baldi is more affordable (~$35–45) and has more pools. Both are excellent.

Alternative
  • Free hot springs at Río Cholín (natural, no crowds) (~1 hr)
  • Kayaking or SUP on Lago Arenal (~2 hr)

Day 7 Drive to Tamarindo — Pacific Beach Town La Fortuna → Tamarindo
Morning
  • Check out of hotel and load car (~30 min)
  • Drive La Fortuna → Tamarindo via Cañas (~3.5–4 hr, 230 km)
  • Most of the drive is on paved Pan-American Highway
Afternoon
  • Check in to hotel near Tamarindo beach (~20 min)
  • First walk on Tamarindo beach (~1 hr)
  • Rent a surfboard or take a beginner surf lesson (~1.5 hr)
Evening
  • Sunset on the beach — Tamarindo has stunning Pacific sunsets (~45 min)
  • Dinner at El Coconut or Nogui's (~1.5 hr)
  • Evening drinks at Aqua Disco or Patagonia Bar (~1–2 hr)

Day 9 Final Morning + Return to San José Tamarindo → San José
Morning
  • Sunrise beach walk or final swim (~1 hr)
  • Breakfast and last look at Tamarindo (~45 min)
  • Check out of hotel and pack car (~30 min)
Drive Back
  • Drive Tamarindo → SJO Airport via Route 1 & 27 (~4–4.5 hr, 280 km)
  • Return rental car at SJO — allow 1–1.5 hr before flight

⏰ Flight Tip: Allow plenty of buffer time. Traffic entering San José can be slow, especially in the afternoon. Schedule flights for late afternoon or evening if possible.

Alternative
  • Fly Tamarindo → SJO (Sansa Airlines, ~35 min) instead of driving ($80–130)
