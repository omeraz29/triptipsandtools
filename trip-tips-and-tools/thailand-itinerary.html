🏙 Bangkok — Days 1–4
Day 1 Arrival + Temples + Rooftop Bars Bangkok
Arrival
  • Land at Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK) (allow 1–2 hr for immigration)
  • Take the Airport Rail Link to city center (~30 min, ฿45)
  • Check into hotel and freshen up (~30 min)
Afternoon
  • Wat Pho — Temple of the Reclining Buddha, one of Bangkok's oldest temples (~1.5 hr)
  • Wat Arun — Temple of Dawn across the Chao Phraya River (~1 hr)
  • Take the cross-river ferry between the two temples (~5 min, ฿5)

Dress Code: Cover shoulders and knees at all temples. Sarongs are available to borrow at the entrance if needed.

Evening
  • Dinner at a riverside restaurant along the Chao Phraya (~1.5 hr)
  • Sunset drinks at a rooftop bar — Lebua Sky Bar or Vertigo at Banyan Tree (~1.5 hr)

Day 2 Grand Palace + Chatuchak + Street Food Bangkok
Morning
  • Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew — Bangkok's most iconic landmark (~2 hr)
  • Home of the Emerald Buddha — arrive early to beat the crowds
  • Book tickets online in advance to skip queues

⚠ Scam Alert: If anyone near the Grand Palace tells you it's "closed today" and offers to take you somewhere else — ignore them. It's a well-known scam. The palace is open daily.

Afternoon
  • Lunch at a local restaurant near the palace (~1 hr)
  • Chatuchak Weekend Market (Saturdays & Sundays) — one of the world's largest markets (~2 hr)
  • Or Jim Thompson House Museum if not a weekend (~1 hr)
Evening
  • Khao San Road for buzzing nightlife and street food (~2 hr)
  • Try pad thai, mango sticky rice, and fresh coconut ice cream
Alternative
  • Damnoen Saduak Floating Market — best visited early morning (~2 hr from Bangkok)
  • Lumphini Park for a morning jog and monitor lizard spotting (~1 hr)

Day 3 Bangkok Street Food Tour Bangkok
Morning
  • Breakfast at Or Tor Kor Market — Bangkok's top fresh food market (~1 hr)
  • Try khao tom (rice soup), fresh tropical fruits, and Thai iced tea
  • Guided food tour of Chinatown (Yaowarat Road) (~3 hr)

Food Tour Tip: Bangkok has some of the world's best street food. A guided tour is highly recommended — guides take you to hidden vendors locals actually eat at. Tours run ~$30–50/person and cover 8–10 tastings.

Afternoon
  • Eat your way through Chinatown — dim sum, roast duck, fresh seafood (~2 hr)
  • Visit Wat Traimit — Temple of the Golden Buddha (~45 min)
  • Explore the narrow alleys and herb shops of old Chinatown (~1 hr)
Evening
  • Dinner at Gaggan Anand or Bo.lan for upscale Thai cuisine (~2 hr)
  • OR continue the street food crawl along Silom Night Market (~1.5 hr)
  • Try: pad see ew, green curry, tom yum soup, and mango sticky rice
Alternative
  • Thai cooking class — learn to make 4–5 dishes yourself (~3 hr, ~$40)
  • Bike tour of Bang Krachao — Bangkok's green lung island (~3 hr)

Day 4 Ayutthaya Day Trip + Fly to Chiang Mai Bangkok → Chiang Mai
Morning — Ayutthaya
  • Day trip to Ayutthaya — ancient capital ruins, 1.5 hr north of Bangkok (~1.5 hr by train)
  • Bike through the ancient temple complex — Wat Mahathat, Wat Phra Si Sanphet (~3 hr)
  • Iconic headless Buddha statue wrapped in tree roots — not to be missed

Ayutthaya Tip: Rent a bicycle at the train station (~฿50/day) to explore the ruins at your own pace. The entire historic park is flat and easy to cycle.

Afternoon / Evening
  • Return to Bangkok by train (~1.5 hr)
  • Head to Suvarnabhumi or Don Mueang Airport
  • Short flight Bangkok → Chiang Mai (~1 hr)
  • Check into hotel in Chiang Mai (~20 min)
  • Evening stroll around the Old City moat (~1 hr)

🌿 Chiang Mai — Days 5–7
Day 5 Elephant Sanctuary + Old City Temples Chiang Mai
Full Day — Elephant Sanctuary
  • Elephant Sanctuary — spend a full day with rescued elephants in an ethical sanctuary (~6–7 hr)
  • Feed, bathe, and walk alongside elephants in their natural habitat
  • No riding — these are ethical sanctuaries that prioritize elephant welfare
  • Most sanctuaries include transport from Chiang Mai, lunch, and a guide

Top Ethical Sanctuaries: Elephant Nature Park (most well-known), Elephant Jungle Sanctuary, and Ran-Tong Elephant Care. Book well in advance — these sell out weeks ahead. Expect to pay ~$80–120/person.

Evening
  • Chiang Mai Sunday Walking Street (if visiting on Sunday) — incredible local market (~2 hr)
  • Or Nimman Road area for trendy restaurants and bars (~1.5 hr)
  • Try Khao Soi — Chiang Mai's signature coconut curry noodle soup

Day 6 Doi Inthanon + Old City + Night Bazaar Chiang Mai
Morning
  • Doi Inthanon National Park — Thailand's highest peak, stunning waterfalls and hill tribe villages (~1.5 hr drive)
  • Wachirathan Waterfall — one of Thailand's most impressive waterfalls (~45 min)
  • Twin Royal Chedis — pagodas surrounded by manicured gardens at the summit (~45 min)
Afternoon
  • Return to Chiang Mai (~1.5 hr)
  • Explore Chiang Mai Old City — wander the moat-surrounded historic quarter (~1.5 hr)
  • Visit Wat Chedi Luang and Wat Phra Singh — two of the most important temples (~1 hr)
  • Lunch at a local restaurant in the Old City (~1 hr)
Evening
  • Chiang Mai Night Bazaar — one of Thailand's best night markets (~2 hr)
  • Hill tribe handicrafts, silverwork, silk, and street food
  • Dinner and drinks on Loi Kroh Road nearby (~1.5 hr)
Alternative
  • Thai massage — Chiang Mai is famous for its massage schools (~1–2 hr, ~$10–15)
  • Muay Thai boxing match at Thaphae Boxing Stadium (~2 hr)

Day 7 Zipline + Fly to Phi Phi Islands Chiang Mai → Phi Phi Islands
Morning
  • Flight of the Gibbon zipline — world-class zipline through jungle canopy (~3 hr)
  • Over 30 platforms and cables up to 800m long through rainforest
  • One of the best zipline experiences in Southeast Asia — book in advance
Afternoon / Evening
  • Check out of hotel and head to airport (allow 1.5 hr)
  • Fly Chiang Mai → Krabi or Phuket (~1.5 hr)
  • Take a speedboat transfer to Phi Phi Don Island (~1.5–2 hr from Krabi)
  • Check into beachfront resort or guesthouse (~20 min)
  • First evening walk on Phi Phi — explore Tonsai Village and beachfront (~1 hr)

🚤 Ferry Tip: Last ferries from Krabi to Phi Phi typically depart around 4–5 PM. Time your flight accordingly — Phuket has more frequent boat options if you arrive late.

🏝 Phi Phi Islands — Days 8–10
Day 8 Island Hopping + Snorkeling + Maya Bay Phi Phi Islands
Morning
  • Full day speedboat island hopping tour (~6–7 hr)
  • Maya Bay — the iconic beach from The Beach (Leonardo DiCaprio film)
  • Snorkeling at Shark Point — reef sharks, colorful coral, tropical fish
  • Monkey Beach — feed wild monkeys from the boat
  • Pileh Lagoon — stunning emerald-green enclosed lagoon

Maya Bay: Now only accessible by day — swimming is permitted but overnight stays are banned to protect the coral. Book a tour that includes early morning entry to beat the crowds.

Evening
  • Sunset drinks on the beach at Tonsai Bay (~1 hr)
  • Dinner at a beachfront restaurant — fresh grilled seafood is the highlight (~1.5 hr)
  • Fire show on the beach — Phi Phi is famous for nightly fire performances

Day 9 Bioluminescent Plankton Night Snorkel + Beach Day Phi Phi Islands
Morning — Free Beach Day
  • Relax on Long Beach or Loh Dalum Bay (~2 hr)
  • Kayak rental around the island's coastline (~1.5 hr, ~$10)
  • Breakfast and coffee at a beachfront café (~45 min)
Afternoon
  • Hike to Phi Phi Viewpoint — stunning panoramic view over both bays (~1 hr)
  • Snorkeling at Loh Samah Bay — some of the clearest water on the island (~1.5 hr)
  • Late afternoon rest — tonight is a late night! (~1 hr)
Evening — Bioluminescent Plankton
  • Bioluminescent Plankton Night Snorkeling Tour — swim through glowing blue plankton in the dark ocean (~2–3 hr)
  • Best experienced on darker nights — avoid full moon periods for maximum glow
  • Tour departs after dark, typically 8–9 PM
  • One of the most magical natural experiences in Southeast Asia

Bioluminescence Tip: The plankton glow electric blue when disturbed by movement in the water. The darker and calmer the night, the more spectacular the display. Book through your hotel or a local tour operator (~$25–40/person).

Day 10 Final Morning + Ferry + Departure Phi Phi Islands → Krabi / Phuket
Morning
  • Final sunrise on the beach (~45 min)
  • Last breakfast at a beachfront spot (~45 min)
  • Check out of hotel and pack bags (~30 min)
Departure
  • Take morning ferry to Krabi or Phuket (~1.5–2 hr)
  • Transfer to Krabi International Airport (KBV) or Phuket Airport (HKT)
  • Allow 2–3 hr before international departure

⏰ Departure Tip: Ferries from Phi Phi run on a schedule — book your ferry in advance to guarantee your seat, especially in high season (November–April). Missing the ferry means missing your flight.

Alternative
  • Extend by adding Koh Lanta (1 hr ferry from Phi Phi) for a quieter beach experience
  • Stop in Krabi town for a half day before your flight
