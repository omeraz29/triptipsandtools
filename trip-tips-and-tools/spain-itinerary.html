🏙 Barcelona — Days 1–4
Day 1 Arrival + Gothic Quarter + Waterfront Barcelona
Arrival
  • Land at Barcelona–El Prat Airport (BCN) (~45 min to city center)
  • Take the Aerobus or metro to city center (~35–45 min, €6–10)
  • Check into hotel and freshen up (~30 min)
Afternoon
  • Walk Las Ramblas — Barcelona's iconic boulevard (~1 hr)
  • Explore the Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic) — narrow medieval streets (~1.5 hr)
  • Visit Barcelona Cathedral (~30 min)
Evening
  • Walk to Barceloneta Beach for sunset (~1 hr)
  • Dinner at a chiringuito (beachfront bar) or El Born neighborhood (~1.5 hr)
  • Evening drinks in El Born — Barcelona's trendiest nightlife area

Tip: Spaniards eat dinner late — most restaurants don't fill up until 9–10 PM. Embrace it!

Day 2 Gaudí Day — Sagrada Família + Park Güell Barcelona
Morning
  • Sagrada Família — Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece basilica (~2 hr)
  • Book timed entry tickets in advance — sells out weeks ahead
  • Tower access recommended for panoramic city views

Must Book: Sagrada Família tickets sell out well in advance. Book online at sagradafamilia.org as soon as your dates are confirmed.

Afternoon
  • Lunch in the Gràcia neighborhood — charming local vibe (~1 hr)
  • Park Güell — Gaudí's colorful mosaic park with city views (~1.5 hr)
  • Book timed entry for the monumental zone in advance
Evening
  • Passeig de Gràcia stroll — see Casa Batlló and Casa Milà (La Pedrera) from outside (~1 hr)
  • Tapas dinner — try patatas bravas, pan con tomate, jamón (~1.5 hr)
Alternative
  • Casa Batlló interior tour (stunning but pricey ~€35) (~1 hr)
  • La Pedrera rooftop tour at sunset (~1 hr)

Day 3 Montjuïc + Picasso Museum + Nightlife Barcelona
Morning
  • Take cable car or funicular up to Montjuïc (~20 min)
  • Montjuïc Castle — panoramic views over the city and port (~1 hr)
  • Jardins de Laribal — terraced gardens on the hillside (~45 min)
Afternoon
  • Lunch in El Born neighborhood (~1 hr)
  • Picasso Museum — one of the most important Picasso collections in the world (~1.5 hr)
  • Stroll Santa Maria del Mar church and El Born market (~1 hr)
Evening
  • Sunset at Bunkers del Carmel — the best 360° view in Barcelona (~1.5 hr)
  • Dinner and bar hop in El Raval or Barceloneta (~2 hr)
  • Barcelona nightlife starts late — clubs don't fill until 1–2 AM
Alternative
  • FC Barcelona Camp Nou stadium tour (~1.5 hr)
  • Day trip to Montserrat mountain monastery (~half day)

Day 4 Beach Day + Fly to Palma de Mallorca Barcelona → Palma de Mallorca
Morning
  • Final morning at Barceloneta Beach (~2 hr)
  • Lunch near the beach or La Boqueria market (~1 hr)
  • Check out of hotel and head to airport (allow 2 hr)
Afternoon / Evening
  • Short flight Barcelona → Palma de Mallorca (~50 min)
  • Check into hotel in Palma (~30 min)
  • Evening walk along Palma's stunning waterfront promenade (~1 hr)
  • Dinner in the old town — try ensaïmada pastry and sobrassada (~1.5 hr)

✈ Flight Tip: Vueling, Iberia, and Ryanair all fly Barcelona → Palma frequently. Book in advance for best prices — flights can be as low as €30–50.

🏝 Palma de Mallorca — Days 5–7
Day 5 Palma Old Town + Cathedral + Beaches Palma de Mallorca
Morning
  • Catedral de Mallorca (La Seu) — stunning Gothic cathedral overlooking the sea (~1 hr)
  • Almudaina Royal Palace next door (~45 min)
  • Wander the Arab Quarter and old town streets (~1 hr)
Afternoon
  • Lunch at a terrace restaurant in the old town (~1 hr)
  • Playa de Palma or Cala Major beach (~2 hr)
  • Rent a boat or join a sailing tour around the bay (~2 hr)
Evening
  • Sunset drinks at a rooftop bar overlooking the cathedral (~1 hr)
  • Dinner in Santa Catalina neighborhood — Palma's foodie district (~1.5 hr)

Day 6 Tramuntana Mountains + Hidden Coves Mallorca
Morning — Mountains
  • Rent a car for the day — essential for exploring the island (~€40–60/day)
  • Drive the scenic Serra de Tramuntana mountain road (UNESCO World Heritage) (~1.5 hr drive)
  • Stop at Valldemossa village — charming stone streets and Chopin museum (~1 hr)
  • Deià village — one of the most beautiful villages in Spain (~45 min)
Afternoon — Coves
  • Cala de Deià — small hidden cove accessible by walking path (~1.5 hr)
  • OR Cala Sóller — larger beach near the charming town of Sóller (~2 hr)
  • Lunch at a beachside restaurant (~1 hr)
Evening
  • Drive back to Palma via Sóller (~45 min)
  • Dinner back in Palma (~1.5 hr)
Alternative
  • Cap de Formentor — dramatic cliff lighthouse at the northern tip (~2 hr drive)
  • Caves of Drach (Coves del Drac) — stunning underground lake (~1.5 hr)

Day 7 Boat Day + Fly to Madrid Mallorca → Madrid
Morning
  • Full day boat charter or catamaran tour around the island (~4–5 hr)
  • Snorkeling, cliff jumping, swimming in crystal clear coves
  • Most tours include lunch and drinks on board

Boat Tours: Half-day and full-day catamaran tours are popular and affordable (~€50–80/person). Book in advance especially in summer — they fill up fast.

Afternoon / Evening
  • Check out of hotel and head to Palma airport (allow 2 hr)
  • Flight Palma → Madrid (~1 hr)
  • Check into hotel in Madrid (~30 min)
  • First night stroll around Puerta del Sol and Plaza Mayor (~1 hr)
  • Late dinner — Madrid has the best nightlife in Spain (~1.5 hr)

🏛 Madrid — Days 8–10
Day 8 Prado Museum + Retiro Park + Tapas Crawl Madrid
Morning
  • Museo del Prado — one of the world's greatest art museums (~2.5 hr)
  • Must-sees: Velázquez's Las Meninas, Goya's Black Paintings, El Greco
  • Book tickets online to skip the queue
Afternoon
  • Lunch near the museum (~1 hr)
  • Retiro Park — Madrid's stunning central park (~1.5 hr)
  • Rent a rowboat on the lake (~45 min)
  • Crystal Palace (Palacio de Cristal) inside the park (~30 min)
Evening — Tapas Crawl
  • La Latina neighborhood — best tapas bars in Madrid (~2–3 hr)
  • Must try: Calle Cava Baja for bar hopping
  • Order wine or vermouth and tapas at each stop

Day 9 Royal Palace + Bernabéu + Rooftop Bars Madrid
Morning
  • Royal Palace of Madrid — the largest royal palace in Europe by floor area (~1.5 hr)
  • Sabatini Gardens and Almudena Cathedral next door (~45 min)
  • Plaza de España and Gran Vía stroll (~1 hr)
Afternoon
  • Lunch in Malasaña neighborhood — Madrid's bohemian district (~1 hr)
  • Estadio Santiago Bernabéu — Real Madrid stadium tour (~1.5 hr)
  • Mercado de San Miguel — covered food market near Plaza Mayor (~1 hr)
Evening
  • Sunset drinks on a rooftop bar — Círculo de Bellas Artes has the best views (~1 hr)
  • Dinner in Chueca or Malasaña (~1.5 hr)
  • Madrid nightlife — the city truly never sleeps (~2+ hr)
Alternative
  • Reina Sofía Museum — Picasso's Guernica + modern art (~2 hr)
  • Day trip to Toledo — medieval walled city 30 min by train (~half day)

Day 10 Final Morning + Departure Madrid
Morning
  • Churros con chocolate at Chocolatería San Ginés — Madrid's most iconic breakfast (~45 min)
  • Final stroll around Puerta del Sol and Plaza Mayor (~1 hr)
  • Last minute shopping on Gran Vía or El Rastro flea market (Sundays only) (~1 hr)
Departure
  • Check out and head to Adolfo Suárez Madrid–Barajas Airport (MAD) (~30–45 min by metro)
  • Allow 2.5 hr before flight for international departures

⏰ Airport Tip: Madrid's airport has 4 terminals — make sure you know which one your flight departs from. T4 (most international) and T1–3 are a short bus ride apart.

Alternative
  • Day trip to Segovia — Roman aqueduct and fairytale castle (~1 hr by train)
  • Visit Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum for a more manageable art experience (~1.5 hr)
