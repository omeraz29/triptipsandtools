Cusco — Days 1–4
Day 1 Arrival Cusco — Acclimatize + Old City Exploration Cusco
Arrival
  • Fly into Alejandro Velasco Astete International Airport (CUZ) (~1.5 hr from Lima)
  • Check into hotel and rest — acclimatization is essential (~2 hr)
  • Cusco sits at 3,400m (11,150 ft) — altitude sickness is real, take it slow on Day 1

⚠ Altitude Warning: Cusco is one of the highest cities in the world. Drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol on Day 1, and consider altitude sickness medication (Acetazolamide) prescribed before your trip. Most people feel fine after 24–48 hrs.

Afternoon
  • Gentle walk around Plaza de Armas — Cusco's stunning main square (~1 hr)
  • Cusco Cathedral — one of the most impressive Spanish colonial cathedrals in the Americas (~1 hr)
  • Qorikancha — Inca Temple of the Sun, partially preserved beneath a colonial church (~45 min)

Coca Tea: Offered everywhere in Cusco — locals swear by it for altitude sickness. It's made from coca leaves, legal in Peru, and genuinely helps with acclimatization.

Evening
  • Light dinner in San Blas neighborhood — Cusco's bohemian artisan quarter (~1.5 hr)
  • Try lomo saltado, ceviche, or quinoa soup — take it easy on food portions tonight
  • Early rest — big days ahead

Day 2 Sacsayhuamán + San Blas + Inca Ruins Cusco
Morning
  • Sacsayhuamán — massive Inca fortress overlooking Cusco, 30 min walk uphill from the plaza (~2 hr)
  • Enormous stone walls — some blocks weigh over 100 tons, baffling engineers to this day
  • Panoramic views over Cusco from the top
  • Also visit Qenqo, Puka Pukara, and Tambomachay on the same circuit (~1.5 hr combined)
Afternoon
  • Lunch in San Blas — try a set lunch menu (menú del día) for $3–5 (~1 hr)
  • Explore San Blas neighborhood — whitewashed walls, artisan workshops, narrow cobblestone streets (~1.5 hr)
  • San Blas church — stunning carved pulpit considered one of the finest in the Americas (~30 min)
Evening
  • Dinner at Cicciolina or MAP Café — two of Cusco's best restaurants (~1.5 hr)
  • Try pisco sour — Peru's national cocktail

Boleto Turístico: The tourist ticket (~$40) covers Sacsayhuamán and most Cusco area ruins. Worth buying if you plan to visit multiple sites — available at the COSITUC office near Plaza de Armas.

Day 3 Sacred Valley — Pisac + Ollantaytambo Sacred Valley
Morning
  • Hire a private driver or join a tour from Cusco to the Sacred Valley (~1.5 hr drive)
  • Pisac Ruins — dramatic hilltop Inca citadel with terraced agriculture and stunning valley views (~2 hr)
  • Pisac Market below the ruins — one of Peru's best artisan markets for textiles and crafts (~1 hr)
Afternoon
  • Lunch at a local restaurant in the valley (~1 hr)
  • Ollantaytambo — the best-preserved Inca town in Peru, still inhabited since Inca times (~2 hr)
  • Massive sun temple fortress — steep terraced walls rising dramatically above the town
  • Walk the original Inca streets through the living town below the ruins
Optional Stops
  • Moray — mysterious circular Inca agricultural terraces (~45 min)
  • Maras salt mines — thousands of terraced salt pools still harvested today (~30 min)
Evening
  • Return to Cusco by evening (~1.5 hr)
  • Dinner in the Plaza de Armas area (~1.5 hr)

Day 4 Rainbow Mountain Vinicunca
Full Day
  • Depart Cusco early — most tours leave by 4:30–5 AM (~3 hr drive to trailhead)
  • Rainbow Mountain (Vinicunca) — multicolored mineral stripes across a 5,200m mountain face (~4–5 hr hiking)
  • Colors range from deep reds to yellows, greens, and purples — genuinely breathtaking
  • Lunch and snacks included with most tours

⚠ Altitude + Fitness: This is a strenuous hike at very high altitude. Horses available along the trail if needed (~$15). Not recommended for those with heart or respiratory conditions.

Best Time: May–October is dry season and the best time to hike. Always bring layers as temperatures drop dramatically at altitude even in summer.

Evening
  • Return to Cusco by late afternoon (~3 hr drive)
  • Rest and recovery dinner — you've earned it (~1.5 hr)
  • Pack for Machu Picchu tomorrow

Machu Picchu — Days 5–7
Day 5 Train to Aguas Calientes + First Visit to Machu Picchu Cusco → Aguas Calientes
Morning
  • Check out of Cusco hotel and transfer to Ollantaytambo train station (~1.5 hr)
  • Board the PeruRail or Inca Rail train to Aguas Calientes (~1.5 hr)
  • Stunning journey through the Sacred Valley, cloud forest, and jungle as you descend in altitude

Train Booking: Book as early as possible — they sell out months ahead. The Vistadome train has panoramic windows and is worth the upgrade. Both PeruRail and Inca Rail are reliable.

Afternoon
  • Arrive Aguas Calientes and check into hotel (~20 min)
  • Take the afternoon bus up to Machu Picchu for a first visit (~30 min bus)
  • First look at Machu Picchu — take your time exploring the citadel (~3 hr)
  • Classic viewpoint at the Sun Gate trail entrance for the iconic photo
Evening
  • Return to Aguas Calientes by bus (~30 min)
  • Dinner along the main street — fresh trout from the local rivers is a specialty (~1.5 hr)
  • Aguas Calientes hot springs — natural thermal baths perfect after hiking (~1 hr)
  • Early rest — sunrise at Machu Picchu tomorrow

Day 6 Machu Picchu — Sunrise + Full Exploration Machu Picchu
Sunrise — Must Not Miss
  • Wake up at 4:30 AM — catch the first bus up at 5:30 AM (~30 min bus)
  • Sunrise at Machu Picchu — clouds roll through the ruins as the sun rises over the mountains
  • One of the most extraordinary sights in the world — this is the moment you came for
  • Arrive before the crowds — the site fills up by 9–10 AM

Entry is strictly timed and limited. Book tickets months ahead at machupicchu.gob.pe — slots sell out fast. Choose Circuit 1 (classic overview) for your first visit. A licensed guide (~$20–30) is highly recommended.

Morning — Full Exploration
  • Temple of the Sun — the finest stonework in all of Machu Picchu (~30 min)
  • Intihuatana stone — the sacred "hitching post of the sun" used for astronomical purposes (~20 min)
  • Temple of the Three Windows — one of the most photographed spots (~20 min)
  • Agricultural terraces — stunning farming platforms carved into the mountainside (~45 min)
  • Sun Gate (Inti Punku) trail — 45 min hike for the classic panoramic view (~1.5 hr round trip)
Afternoon
  • Lunch at Tinkuy Buffet — the only restaurant inside the ruins (~1 hr)
  • Huayna Picchu mountain — short steep climb for aerial views of the citadel (~2 hr — book separately)
  • Return to Aguas Calientes by afternoon bus (~30 min)
Alternative
  • Inca Bridge trail — short hike to a suspension bridge built into a cliff face (~45 min)
  • Machu Picchu Mountain — longer climb with wider views than Huayna Picchu (~3 hr)

Lima — Days 8–10
Day 8 Fly to Lima + Miraflores + Pacific Cliffs Cusco → Lima
Morning
  • Check out and fly Cusco → Lima (~1.5 hr flight)
  • Check into hotel in Miraflores — Lima's best neighborhood for visitors (~30 min)
Afternoon
  • Walk the Malecón — Lima's stunning clifftop promenade above the Pacific Ocean (~1.5 hr)
  • Parque del Amor — lovers' park on the cliff edge with great ocean views (~30 min)
  • Paragliding off the Miraflores cliffs — one of the most accessible places in the world to try it (~30 min flight, ~$60–80)
Evening
  • Dinner in Miraflores — Lima is one of the best food cities in the world (~2 hr)
  • Must try: fresh ceviche, tiradito, causa, and anticuchos (grilled beef heart skewers)
  • Top restaurants: Central, Maido, or La Mar for world-class Peruvian cuisine

Lima Food Scene: Lima consistently ranks among the world's top food cities. Don't skip the ceviche — Peruvian ceviche is cured in fresh lime juice with aji amarillo chili. La Mar is a great accessible option that doesn't require months of advance booking.

Day 9 Historic Lima + Barranco + Seafood Lima
Morning
  • Historic Centre (Centro Histórico) — UNESCO World Heritage Site (~2.5 hr)
  • Plaza Mayor — Lima's grand main square with the Government Palace and Cathedral
  • Convento de San Francisco — stunning colonial monastery with famous catacombs (~1 hr)
  • Palacio de Torre Tagle — finest example of Spanish baroque architecture in Lima (~20 min)
Afternoon
  • Lunch at Mercado Surquillo — Lima's best local food market (~1 hr)
  • Try fresh ceviche, empanadas, and tropical fruits at the market stalls
  • Barranco neighborhood — Lima's bohemian seaside district, full of color and street art (~2 hr)
  • Bridge of Sighs (Puente de los Suspiros) — picturesque colonial bridge with ocean views (~20 min)
Evening
  • Sunset drinks at a clifftop bar in Barranco (~1 hr)
  • Dinner in Barranco — great restaurant scene, more relaxed than Miraflores (~1.5 hr)
  • Live music and pisco bars — Barranco is Lima's nightlife hub (~1–2 hr)
Alternative
  • Larco Museum — world-class pre-Columbian artifacts in a stunning colonial mansion (~1.5 hr)
  • Huaca Pucllana — Wari culture pyramid ruins right in the middle of Miraflores (~1 hr)

Day 10 Final Morning + Departure Lima
Morning
  • Final breakfast — try pan con chicharrón (crispy pork sandwich) at a local bakery (~45 min)
  • Last walk along the Malecón cliffs — the Pacific at sunrise is stunning (~1 hr)
  • Last minute shopping — Indian Market in Miraflores for textiles and crafts (~1 hr)
Departure
  • Transfer to Jorge Chávez International Airport (LIM) (~30–45 min from Miraflores)
  • Allow 3 hr before international departure — Lima airport can be busy

⏰ Airport Tip: Lima's airport is about 45 min from Miraflores in normal traffic — but Lima traffic can be unpredictable. Give yourself plenty of time, especially for morning departures.

Alternative
  • Larco Herrera Museum if you missed it earlier — absolutely world class (~1.5 hr)
  • Miraflores waterfront for a final pisco sour at a clifftop bar
